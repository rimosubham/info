The beginning.

In April 2023, Arjun and Priya came back from a nine-day "curated cultural journey" across Rajasthan. They'd paid ₹84,000 each. They'd seen four cities, three forts, two gimmicky cooking classes, and zero people they'd remember a year later. On the flight back they did what bitter people often do — complained for two hours.

By the landing, the complaint had turned into a list. By the following week, the list had turned into a spreadsheet. By the following month, we had 31 places across four Indian states that we'd actually lived in, or had friends living in, and a simple idea:

"What if a travel company only ever sold what it knew?"

We registered the domain that weekend. We didn't know what we were doing. We still don't, most days. But every place we've listed since then has been walked by at least one of us, and that one rule has kept the business honest.

The name.

Offbeat Peak is a contradiction on purpose. A "peak" is usually the most famous spot — the one everyone photographs, crowds on, posts about. "Offbeat" is the refusal. We wanted a name that promised both — something meaningful, but not something everyone has already been to. Like a peak that not every trekker knows the name of. Those are the best ones.

What we won't do.

We won't add a destination we haven't been to. We won't recommend a homestay we haven't slept at. We won't stuff itineraries with "must-sees" just to make them look full. We won't take commissions from hotel chains that corrupt our recommendations. We won't run the 50-person bus tours that pollute the very villages we love.

We will, however, take responsibility when something goes wrong. We'll rebook your stays if roads close. We'll call a doctor if you need one. We'll sit with you on WhatsApp at 11pm when your flight delayed. These aren't policies. They're what normal people do for each other.

What we're building towards.

Slow, steady, and a little stubborn. We don't want to be the biggest travel brand in India. We want to be the one your trusted friend recommends. The one you come back to three times over five years. The one whose team you've met and whose opinions you respect.

By 2028, we want to have launched 12 destinations properly (Andaman and Nagaland are next). We want every single one to be something we'd send our mothers to. That's the bar.

— Arjun & the Offbeat Peak team

Things we believe

Six rules we don't break.

These aren't mission statements. They're the arguments we've had with each other, distilled into principles. They protect the travelers and the places we care about.

01

Walk before we sell

No listing goes live until someone on the team has spent at least three days there. No exceptions, no shortcuts.

02

Hosts are family

Our homestay hosts are in our phones. They text at 11pm to ask about our mothers. They get paid fairly and on time. Always.

03

Say no to the wrong trip

If your brief doesn't match what we do well, we'll tell you. And send you to someone who does it better. Fit over revenue.

04

Small over big

Max 12 travelers per group. Max 8 homestays per destination. Max 2 destinations launched per year. Scale ruins the thing.

05

Honest pricing

Our margins are posted on every quote. No hidden markup, no "surge" fees, no surprise charges. If it's in the quote, it's the price.

06

Care after the sale

WhatsApp support during every trip. Rebooking at zero cost if things go sideways. We've done it 43 times. Zero travelers stranded.

How we got here

A short timeline.

A bad trip to Rajasthan

Arjun and Priya return from a packaged tour and realize they hate how travel has become. The idea for Offbeat Peak is born on a delayed IndiGo flight.

The first homestay

We meet Asha Ji in Tirthan Valley. She becomes our first homestay host. Her siddu recipe is now the official company comfort food.

Our first 100 travelers

Hit 100 trips sold. Realize we haven't paid ourselves in 8 months. Celebrate by paying ourselves ₹12,000 each.

Aug 2024

Team grows to six

Niharika joins as trip designer. Ravi handles operations. Priya W. leads content. Our WhatsApp groups multiply.

Mar 2025

First group trip booked out

Darjeeling–Sikkim group trip sells its 12 spots in 48 hours. We cry. Then start planning the next one.

Apr 2026

612 travelers. Still small. Still here.

Three years in. Andaman launches in July. Nagaland in December. The plan is to stay small, stay weird, and stay honest.
