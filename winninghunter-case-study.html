The problem

Four problems standing between WinningHunter and growth

A capable product in a hot niche, with no foundation underneath. Every layer of the SEO stack needed work, simultaneously.

01

Spammy, irrelevant backlinks

The existing backlink profile was full of low-quality, off-topic links. Worse than no links: a profile that signaled "low-trust site" to Google and offered nothing for AI crawlers to cite.

02

No topical authority

The blog section was thin and unstructured. Search engines and LLMs had no signal that WinningHunter was a credible voice in product research or ad intelligence.

03

Invisible to AI

Zero citations across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, or Google's AI Overview. Competitors like Pipiads and Ecomhunt were getting cited daily. WinningHunter was being skipped entirely.

04

No founder bandwidth

Roggie is a builder, not a marketer. He didn't have weeks to learn keyword research, outreach playbooks, or PR. He needed someone to run the whole stack and report results.

The strategy

Four campaigns. Run in parallel. Foundation-up.

When the entire stack is broken, you can't optimize. You rebuild from the ground up, in the right order. Click each tab to see the playbook.

Foundation

Topical Authority Build-Out

You can't earn AI citations or quality backlinks if your own site doesn't read as an authority. Step one was making WinningHunter the most useful resource in its niche.

  • Blog architecture overhaul: rebuilt the blog section with a clear topical hierarchy around product research, ad spy, and dropshipping winning products.
  • Keyword research from scratch: mapped every commercial and informational query in the niche. Prioritized by intent and difficulty.
  • Pillar + cluster model: deep pillar pages on core topics, with supporting cluster articles that interlinked correctly.
  • Why this matters: AI tools cite sites that demonstrate clear topical depth. No depth, no citations.
AI focus

Manual AI Query Hunting

Most agencies "hope" for AI mentions. We targeted them directly, query by query, the same way we'd target a backlink.

  • Query mapping: identified the exact queries in our niche where AI tools surface results, and which placements get cited.
  • Listicle prioritization: focused outreach on listicle articles, especially those ranking in the top 3 positions, which are disproportionately cited by AI.
  • Featured platform alerts: used Featured to monitor real-time queries from journalists and writers in our niche, responding fast with quotable insights.
  • Manual outreach to citation-worthy pages: if a page was getting AI-cited and didn't mention WinningHunter, we pitched the writer to add it.
PR leverage

The .edu PR Stack

A two-step play. Land an .edu placement first. Then leverage that .edu link as a credibility signal in PR pitches to industry publications.

  • Step 1, .edu guest posts: wrote useful, niche-relevant content for .edu blogs that accept contributor articles. Slow to land, but the credibility multiplier is enormous.
  • Step 2, PR campaigns referencing .edu: when pitching journalists and bloggers, led with "as featured on [.edu institution]" to instantly elevate trust.
  • Conversion lift: PR response rates roughly tripled once we had .edu credibility to reference.
  • Compounding effect: each new tier of placement opened doors to the next, .edu opened mid-tier media, mid-tier media opened larger publications.
Reverse-engineering

Competitor Reverse-Engineering & Unlinked Mentions

We didn't guess what would work. We looked at exactly what was working for competitors and intercepted it.

  • AI ranking analysis: reverse-engineered which queries Pipiads, Ecomhunt, and others were ranking on across AI tools. Targeted those exact pages.
  • Placement interception: for every page citing a competitor, evaluated whether WinningHunter could be added as a comparable. If yes, pitched.
  • Unlinked brand mentions: tracked where WinningHunter was mentioned but not linked, then asked the writer to add the link, easy wins, high relevance.
  • Existing relationships leverage: tapped into our network of website admins from prior work to fast-track placements that would have taken months otherwise.
