The constraints

Four constraints that made this the hardest brief I'd ever worked on

Every other engagement gives you tools. This one took them away. Each constraint forced new tactics that didn't exist in my previous playbook.

01

No link exchanges allowed

My biggest tactic across every prior engagement was off the table. Canva had a strict policy: no reciprocal link arrangements, no ABC swaps, no quid pro quo. Every link had to be earned without offering anything in return.

02

A brand-new domain

canva.in launched with zero authority. While canva.com had a DR in the high 90s, the new TLD started from absolute scratch. Authority had to be built, not transferred, while users globally still defaulted to .com.

03

12+ regional languages

India isn't a single market. After English, Hindi is widely spoken, but the country runs on Bengali, Marathi, Telugu, Tamil, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Punjabi, Odia, and others. Outreach had to span all of them.

04

Authority signals to Google

For Google to treat canva.in as the authoritative India destination (and not a low-trust regional clone), the link profile had to look like a credible India-native brand from day one. No spam, no shortcuts, no offshore link farms.

The strategy

Four playbooks I had to invent from scratch

When you can't use exchanges and the domain has zero authority, every comfortable tactic disappears. Click each tab to see the new playbooks I had to develop.

Unconventional

Telephonic Outreach to Local Magazines

Most link builders email. The Indian regional press doesn't always respond to email, especially in tier-2 and tier-3 cities. So I picked up the phone.

  • Direct calls to editorial desks: reached out to regional magazines and news sites by phone, in the local language where possible, to introduce canva.in and its India focus.
  • Trust-building before pitch: first calls were never about a link. They were about "we're launching an India experience, we'd love your feedback." The link came later, naturally.
  • Why it worked: regional editors get pitched 50 emails a day from spammers. A phone call from a real human stood out. Conversion rates were 5-10× email outreach.
  • Slow but compounding: each successful call seeded a relationship. The same editors came back with follow-up coverage as canva.in grew.
No exchange = creative

Brand Mention Plays Instead of Links

Without link exchanges, every link had to be a one-way gift from the publisher. So I shifted the ask: not "link to us," but "mention us when relevant." Brand mentions on authority sites still drive trust, citations, and organic discovery, even without a hyperlink.

  • Direct outreach to authors/content writers: bypassed editorial inboxes and went to writers directly. "If you're writing about design tools, would you mention Canva?"
  • Mention-first, link-second: a brand mention without a link still elevates the article's relevance to AI crawlers and Google's entity graph. A link upgrade often followed organically.
  • Lower barrier, higher acceptance: writers who said no to "add a link" said yes to "mention us in this list of relevant tools." Conversion went up dramatically.
  • Long-tail effect: mentions on authority sites like Forbes, BigCommerce, and DigitalOcean compound: AI tools start citing the article, our brand gets cited too.
Strategic

Competitor Reverse-Engineering

Canva has plenty of well-funded competitors with strong India presence: design tools, photo editors, AI image generators. I mined every one of their backlink profiles for the specifically India-relevant placements.

  • Backlink profile audits: systematically pulled every competitor's backlinks via Ahrefs, filtered for India-based or India-relevant publishers.
  • Topical fit analysis: categorized each competitor placement by intent: design tutorials, tool comparisons, listicles, "best of" guides.
  • Identical-fit pitching: for every page where a competitor was listed, evaluated whether canva.in could be added or substituted. If yes, pitched the writer with concrete reasoning.
  • The result: caught up to and surpassed several competitors on India-specific backlinks within 9 months.
Geo-specific

Multilingual Regional Push

For canva.in to be seen as truly India-native, the link profile needed regional language coverage, not just English. This meant outreach in Hindi, Bengali, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, and other major scripts.

  • Regional blog mapping: built a database of 500+ regional language blogs, magazines, and online publications across 10+ Indian languages.
  • Native-language pitching: outreach in the publisher's own language wherever possible. Native-language pitches converted 3× English ones.
  • Topical relevance focus: pitched regional design, education, and creator-focused publications. canva.in fit naturally into their content.
  • Authority signal to Google: a multilingual backlink profile with native-language anchor text told Google: this is a legitimate India brand, not an offshore clone.
