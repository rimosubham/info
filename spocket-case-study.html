The problem

Four challenges holding Spocket back

Despite a stellar brand and a polished product, the off-page SEO foundation was weak, and competitors like Zendrop, AutoDS, and Doba were pulling ahead.

01

Bleeding budget to vendors

Spocket was paying agencies and freelancers 4 to 5× the cost of what equivalent placements should have run, for results that weren't moving the needle.

02

Low-quality backlink profile

2.7K referring domains, DR 67, AS 44. Well below where a brand of Spocket's size and global user base should sit. The ratio of links to referring domains screamed "low diversity."

03

Hyper-competitive niche

Direct competitors like Zendrop, AutoDS, and Doba were sitting on stronger backlink profiles and dominating the SERPs. Catching up needed both speed and quality, simultaneously.

04

Zero AI surface area

Competitors were getting mentioned across AI tools. Spocket had next to no presence, even competitors with weaker backlink profiles were winning AI mentions.

The strategy

Four campaigns. Run in parallel. No paid placements.

Click each tab to see exactly how each campaign worked. Every approach was designed to be replicable, organic, and free.

Main focus

Slack & Email Outreach

The core engine. Direct, personal outreach to niche communities and site owners, building real relationships that converted into placements.

  • Niche Slack communities: joined 30+ e-commerce, marketing, and SaaS communities. Contributed first, pitched later.
  • Reciprocal exchanges: ABC-style swaps where three sites exchanged links. Zero cost, real authority.
  • Personalized email outreach: every email referenced something specific about the recipient's site or recent post.
  • Long-term relationships: the same partners came back month after month with new opportunities.
Secondary focus

Backlink Gap Analysis

Used Ahrefs and similar tools to identify every site that linked to a competitor but not to Spocket, then prioritized the highest-authority, most-relevant gaps.

  • Competitor mining: analyzed Zendrop, AutoDS, Doba backlink profiles for missed opportunities.
  • Authority filter: only chased sites above DR 50 with relevant traffic.
  • Relevance over volume: a single placement on a high-fit site outperformed 10 generic links.
  • Quick wins: "if they linked to a competitor for X reason, here's why we're a better fit" type pitches. Direct and specific.
Ongoing

Broken Link & PR Mentions

Found broken or outdated links on high-profile pages where Spocket could serve as the perfect replacement, and earned PR-style brand mentions in the process.

  • Broken link recovery: identified dead links on authority pages, pitched Spocket as the replacement.
  • Mention tracking: used Mention.com to monitor competitor placements. Wherever they were mentioned without a link, we pitched Spocket as a comparable alternative.
  • PR-style shout-outs: earned brand mentions in industry roundups, "best dropshipping tools" lists, and tutorial content.
  • Continuous loop: new mentions surfaced weekly as competitors got covered.
Hidden gem

The Drafts Strategy

The tactic most link builders miss. Many authority sites don't accept link requests directly, but their writers post drafts in communities for review before publishing.

  • Community monitoring: tracked writer drafts across Slack, Reddit, and niche forums.
  • Helpful suggestions: proposed Spocket as a relevant addition only where it genuinely fit the article.
  • Pre-publish placement: got linked before the article ever went live. No editorial battle required.
  • Compounding effect: writers remembered the helpful suggestion and brought Spocket up unprompted in future drafts.
